# UNCHAOSS
## What

Think of these as hardware and software projects that you can modify and build on to fill that "last mile gap" between software/websites and your own needs. For instance you will find code (TBD) to download bank statements where you can fill in your own banks's details and run.

## Why

"Grand Uncle CB, why did people ever take jobs?"

It was a sunny winter day at that Grand Opening of the Museum of Corporations at Denver Colorado on 29th November 2039. It had been just a few years since the world's last corporation, Mathamota Inc had closed it's doors and indeed, who could ever forget the poignant speech by CEO Ahamorimori as he extolled what Coporations had done for humanity?

The old man was ecstatic. He had, as he told me, waited decades to see this day! Around us people thronged to see the shows - the re-enactments of layoff announcements with their realistics screams, the figures dressed as CEOs and CFOs fisticuffing at "Board Meetings", the Accountant-vs-Auditor "Find the slush fund" games, the Vaporware Startup contests!

And all around me were kids my age asking the same question I had just asked.

"Well", he began, "when formal education began at the time of the Roman Empire, you know that books were super expensive and educating a professional was as costly as is designing a spaceship today". 

I knew what he meant. In those days there were no printing presses. Each textbook had to be written by hand.

"So", he continued, "there was no alternative but to train a few managers and let them guide everyone else. The Romans invented the Corporate hierarchy. They also solved the problem of seasonal labor; back then, during winter, laborers could not be paid - since there was nothing to pay them with - and would leave. But the massive Roman Empire was able to ship produce from, say, Africa to pay labor in France and they were able to invent the system of paying a regular salary - the word is derived from the Roman word salarium".

"That system", he continued, "continued pretty much on autopilot for the next few centuries. The invention of printing allowed education of the masses, but educated individuals were only allowed to maximize a single skill. They 'majored' in that skill and their first job exploited that skill. Eventually a few became managers most of whom did not require any specific skill".

He took a deep breath, his face somber. "The system was massively wasteful. Human beings are born with multiple skills all but one of which was being wasted. Even worse, the system could not scale up to meet the forces unleashed by the Computer Revolution. Skills were becoming obsolesecnt at an ever accelerating pace and it became evident that spending a quarter of a century mastering a skill only to have to obsoleted in ten years was not a workable proposition".

"But luckily", he said, his countenance brightening, "the Computer Revolution brought in a solution as well. As you know realtime adaptive learning - where your brain is scanned while you learn to know what you find difficult, and the teaching adapted to match massively shortened the time to pick up a skill". (I remembered that time when I was desperately trying to understand quadratic equations. The E Tutor had generated a customized animation and suddenly everything made sense)!

"It was a revolution. Where it had cost several years and several hundred thousand dollars to produce a post graduate, suddenly people were becoming '80% masters' in three subjects together in two years and a few hundred dollars. And, all of a sudden, the whole ramshackle, jury-rigged structure of pay scales and org charts no longer made sense to anyone! And so" - he looked around him - "here we are!"

"Grand Uncle CB, tell me about UNCHAOSS!" 

"To tell the truth, when I started building all these products to make my life easier, even as the 'Be a Computer Driver not a Bus Rider' movement was taking off and non developers began leveraging their computers to do all sorts of things - even then I never expected to play any part. Then one day this set of young people approached me and..." his voice trailed off, his mind lost in its own thoughts. I gave him some time, then gently asked "What happened next". 

My only response was a snore. The old man had fallen asleep in the winter sunshine, a contented smile on his face.
